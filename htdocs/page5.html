About this Page

This section of Parasol includes information regarding typhoon signals in the Philippines
This helps viewers gauge the potential danger of an upcoming or nearby typhoon.


Signal 1: Little to No Alarm

  • Winds range from 30 to 60 kilometers per hour. Thus, there is little to no damage at all depending on the structure.

  • Strong, concrete structures experience no damage. Lightweight houses may face some structural damage.

  • Tree branches may snap off; the rest of the tree is otherwise fine. Light trees and plants, however, may tilt.

  • If on their flowering stage, rice crops may face considerable damage due to the strong winds.

  • Evacuation is unlikely, but do so if necessary. Otherwise, stay inside and follow necessary typhoon safety guidelines.

Signal 2: Little to Moderate Alarm

  • Winds range from 61 to 120 kilometers per hour. There is little to moderate damage depending on the structure.

  • Strong, concrete structures experience little to no damage. Lightweight houses may face moderate structural damage.

  • Trees may tilt, break off, or even fall depending on its weight. Crops face considerable damage.

  • Other weak structures such as billboards, electrical posts, and temporary tents may face moderate damage.

  • Only evacuate if necessary. Otherwise, stay inside and follow typhoon safety guidelines.
