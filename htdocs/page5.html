About this Page

This section of Parasol includes information regarding typhoon signals in the Philippines
This helps viewers gauge the potential danger of an upcoming or nearby typhoon.


Signal 1: Little to No Alarm

  • Winds range from 30 to 60 kilometers per hour. Thus, there is little to no damage at all depending on the structure.

  • Strong, concrete structures experience no damage. Lightweight houses may face some structural damage.

  • Tree branches may snap off; the rest of the tree is otherwise fine. Light trees and plants, however, may tilt.

  • If on their flowering stage, rice crops may face considerable damage due to the strong winds.

  • Evacuation is unlikely, but do so if necessary. Otherwise, stay inside and follow necessary typhoon safety guidelines.

Signal 2: Little to Moderate Alarm

  • Winds range from 61 to 120 kilometers per hour. There is little to moderate damage depending on the structure.

  • Strong, concrete structures experience little to no damage. Lightweight houses may face moderate structural damage.

  • Trees may tilt, break off, or even fall depending on its weight. Crops face considerable damage.

  • Other weak structures such as billboards, electrical posts, and temporary tents may face moderate damage.

  • Only evacuate if necessary. Otherwise, stay inside and follow typhoon safety guidelines.

Signal 3: Moderate to High Alarm

  • Winds range from 121 to 170 kilometers per hour. There is light to heavy damage depending on the structure.

  • Strong, concrete structures experience little to moderate damage. Lightweight houses may face heavy structural damage.

  • Many trees may break, tilt, or fall entirely depending on their weight. Crops face considerable damage.

  • Some strong houses, depending on their roofing material, may be unroofed.

  • Prepare as much as possible; follow regular typhoon guidelines albeit more rigidly.

Signal 4: High Alarm

  • Winds range from 171 to 220 kilometers per hour. There is moderate to very heavy depending on the structure.

  • Strong, concrete structures may experience moderate damage. Lightweight houses may face very heavy structural damage.

  • Many trees may break, tilt, or fall entirely depending on their weight. Crops face heavy damage.

  • Large signs and billboards may face damage to the point of total collapse.

  • Prepare as much as possible; follow rigid typhoon guidelines and prepare for possible evacuation.

Signal 5: High to Extreme Alarm

  • Winds measure at 220 kilometers per hour or more. There is heavy to widespread damage depending on the structure.

  • Strong, concrete structures may experience heavy damage. Lightweight houses may face widespread structural damage.

  • Most trees may break, tilt, or fall entirely depending on their weight. Crops face heavy damage.

  • Storm surges and waves measuring up to three meters may heavily affect coastal areas.

  • Prepare as much as possible; follow rigid typhoon guidelines and prepare for likely evacuation.
